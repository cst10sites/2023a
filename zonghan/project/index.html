    What is The Stock Market?

    The stock market is a financial marketplace where publicly traded companies' stocks (shares of ownership of a companie) are bought and sold. It is an important source of capital for companies and it gives investors opportunities to make money through stock performance or dividends (payments made to shareholders from the directors of the company). The stock market is made up of exchanges, such as the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and the NASDAQ, where stocks are listed and traded.

    Stock prices are determined by supply and demand for the shares, with investors willing to pay more for a stock that they believe will increase in value. The stock market can be volatile, meaning that stock prices can fluctuate significantly in a short period of time. However, it has also historically been a source of long-term money growth for investors.
