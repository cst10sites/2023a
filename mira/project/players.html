Borje Salming

Borje Salming was born in Salmi Sweden, he began playing with Swedish teams until he was scouted and asked to join the leafs in 1973. He spent a total of 16 seasons with the Leafs leaving in 1989. Before he joined the leafs, most players thought of european players as to soft to play in north america, Salmings performance helped to permanently eradicate way of thinking.

In 1988 he became the first European player to play in 1,000 NHL games. In 1996 he was inducted into the hall of fame and in 1976, 1977 and 1978 he was Given the viking award, a prize given to the best swedish player in north american teams. At age 71 he was diagnosed with the illness ALS, losing the ability to speak he passed later that year (November 24th 2022).Sadly, Salming never got the chance to raise the Stanley Cup.

Mats Sundin

Mats Sundin was born in 1971 Bromma, Sweden. He played amateur hockey in Sweden until 1984 when moved to Canada and started his NHL career playing for the Quebec Nordiques, he played with them for 4 seasons, from 1984 to 1993, until he was traded to The Maple Leafs in 1994.

He ended up staying with the Leafs for the majority of hi career spending 11 of the seasons as team captain. From early days the fans, players, and administration could tell he would be a star, in only his third season with the leafs, Mats recorded his second highest season of his career , he recorded 41-goals and 94-points. During the 1996-97 season his captain, Doug Gilmour was traded and Mats Sundin was named successor, making him the 16th Maple Leafs Captain and first European Leafs captain ever.

During the 1998-99 season Sundin led his team to the stanley cup playoffs, the made it to the conference finals but were unfortunately defeated by the Buffalo Sabres in 5 games.

Mats Sundin eventually left the Leafs in 2008 and retired in 2009. In 2012 Sundin was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame and in 2013 he was inducted in the IIHF Hall of Fame. He was also awarded the viking award a total of 4 times, making him the only player as of the 2021-2022 season to receive it 4 times.

Tim Horton

Miles Gilbert ‘Tim” Horton was born in 1930 Cochrane Ontario, when he was young, Tim moved around a few times with his family, always staying in ontario and quebec. He developed a passion for hockey and grew up playing in Cochrane, and other small mining towns. He was signed by the Toronto Maple Leafs organization in 1948, and made the move to Toronto to play junior hockey. Two years later in 1950 he turned pro and played for The Leafs’ farm teams. Horton played his first NHL game as a Leaf in 1952, he stayed with them until 1970 winning 4 Stanley cups with them.

Of the ice Tim Horton had other business endeavors, he opened his first of many donut shops in hamilton ontario 1964. Today the Tim Hortons brand has opened over 5 thousand stores in 15 different countries and is valued at 13.99 billion dollars US.

Sadly on the morning of february 21 1974, Tim Horton died at the age of 44. The night before he had played a game in toronto against his former team, then belonging to the Buffalo Sabres. The drunk Tim crashed his sports car after losing control of the vehicle in St. Catharines, Ontario, on his way home to Buffalo.

Even with passing away in 1974, Tim Horton was inducted into the Hall of Fame in 1977 with 518 career points and three All-Star appearances.

Johnny Bower

Johnny Bower was born in Prince Albert Saskatchewan to a Ukrainian-Canadian family, he taught himself to play on his own, using a branch as a stick and malking goalie pads out of an old mattress, at 15 he lied about his age and was enlisted into the canadian army in world war II.

He started playing and proving himself in the AHL, by leading his team to 3 Calder cups championships. Bowers first season in the NHL was with the New York Rangers in 1953-54, in which he was sent back down to the AHL where he stayed for another 4 years. In the 1958 inter-league draft Johnny Bower was chosen by the Toronto Maple Leafs as their starting Goalie. Bower won the Vezina Trophy for the first time in 1961 for conceding the fewest goals in the 1960-61 season.

From 1962 to 1964 he won the STanley Cup for the Maple leafs 3 consecutive times, In 1967 he won the cup for the fourth time which is also the lastone the Leafs have won (as of january 2022). Him and Tony Sawchuck won the Vezina trophy together in 1967. Johnny became the oldest player to play in a stanley cup playoff game at the age of 44 years, 4 months and 29 days. He retired in 1969 due to age and injuries.
