In 1918 the Toronto Maple Leafs (then the Arena Hockey Club of Toronto) won their first Stanley cup with 3 points against 2 in a 5 game final with the Vancouver Millionaires.

In 1967 in a six game series The Toronto Maple Leafs won 4 games to 2 against the Montreal Canadiens. The 1967 Cup was the last one The Leafs have won, making their 56 year drought the longest ever in NHL history, just overtaking the new york rangers and their 54 season drought.

The Maple Leafs experienced major success during the “original six” era, dominating the league and winning 9 Stanley Cups. Second only to Quebec who won 10 of the 25 awarded.

In total the Toronto Maple Leafs have won 13 Stanley Cup Championships making them the second to, once again, The Montreal Canadiens who have won an astonishing 24 times, their last one beingduring the 1992-93 season.
