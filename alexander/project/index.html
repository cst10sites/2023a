Chevrolet Camaro

This 1968 Chevrolet Camaro is owned by Steve Mendenhall. He found the car in a barn situated in Creston, Ohio and bought it, more exactly a rolling shell with a few parts thrown in, for only $4,000, according to Cruisin Times Magazine. Steve wanted a special show car, so he chose Bruce Harvey from Pro Comp Custom in Pittsburg, Pennsylvania to help him create it. The work started with a sectioned body and a chopped roof, followed by a new RideTech ShockWaves front suspension and a Martz Chassis.

The new engine, the LS 427 cid unit that delivers 600 horsepower, was created by Gables Engine & Machine from Akron, Ohio.

Ford Mustang

This Ford 5.0 Mustang GT, owned by the owner of Simon Motorsports, was extremely widened with this brand-new body kit mounted piece by piece by his own company, Simon Motorsports, according to Tuning Blog. The aggressive 20-inch Alpha Male Performance AMP 5 V alloy rims, with their glossy black paint in the middle and the candy-red one on the outer barrels and the lips, look amazing on this car. The rims were wrapped in Michelin Pilot Sport tires that help to get the power of the 5.0-liter NA V8 to the ground.

Nissan 350Z

The owner of this stunning ride, Brian McCann, went through a series of unfortunate events. While working on a personal project car, a Z32, he had to move from Alaska to Arizona, and there, his car was stolen. Thinking in a positive way after the incident, Brian had the courage to try it again, and his car was reincarnated, according to Stance Nation.

The four corners are filled with Forgestar F14 Super Deep Concave rims painted in Candy Red and wrapped in Toyo Proxes tires. In the back, there's a huge FD RX7 diffuser placed on the Do-Luck rear bumper, complementing the large rear wing. There was a lot of work done under the hood, too. The original engine was boosted to deliver 527 to 650 horsepower, depending on the type of fuel added—a true monster that would attract the police in no time.

Toyota Supra

This awesome 1998 Toyota Supra belongs to Christopher Calimlim from Honolulu, Hawaii. Since he's finished his project, this car has competed and won 1st place in almost all of the 12 shows it participated in. Christopher mentioned that there's nothing that could match the iconic shapes of this car or the legendary fourth-generation Supra 2JZ-GTE engine, according to Pas Mag. he managed to replace it with a Boost Logic Stage 2 kit matched with a single 6765 Precision turbo and a GReddy three-row radiator, a blow-off valve, and a Tial wastegate with a four-inch Boost Logic downpipe and exhaust. He also chose a set of Volk G50 19-inch rims and an Air Force air suspension kit to complete the upgrade calendar. Christopher is already planning a few more adjustments and modifications for his ride, and there are also a lot of other car shows he could compete in after he finishes.
